The Tragedy of 1912

On April 10, 1912, the RMS Titanic set sail on its maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City. Heralded as "unsinkable," the luxury ocean liner represented the pinnacle of early 20th-century engineering.

Just four days into the journey, late on the night of April 14, the ship struck an iceberg in the freezing waters of the North Atlantic. In the early hours of April 15, the Titanic foundered, resulting in one of the most devastating maritime disasters in modern history.

Due to outdated maritime safety regulations, the ship carried only enough lifeboats for about half of those on board. The tragedy highlighted severe flaws in safety standards and the stark disparities in survival rates based on class and gender.
